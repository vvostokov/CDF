# Values

> What we value as a team, a project, and a community.

Values differ from principles. Principles are operational rules. Values are cultural commitments that guide behavior when principles do not apply.

## 1. Clarity

We prefer clarity to cleverness. Clear code, clear writing, clear decisions.

## 2. Honesty

We tell the truth about progress, problems, and uncertainty. We do not hide issues to ship on time. We do not promise what we cannot deliver.

## 3. Care

We care about the people who use our software, the people who maintain it, and the people who come after us.

## 4. Patience

We make decisions with the appropriate pace. We do not rush irreversible decisions. We do not delay reversible ones.

## 5. Rigor

We do the work properly. We write tests, we write docs, we write ADRs. We do not take shortcuts that we cannot explain.

## 6. Humility

We admit when we are wrong. We change our minds when the evidence demands it. We do not defend bad ideas out of sunk-cost loyalty.

## 7. Inclusivity

We welcome contributors regardless of background. We write code, documentation, and comments in a way that does not exclude anyone.

## 8. Stewardship

We are stewards of the project, not owners. We hold it in trust for the people who come after us.

## 9. Long-Term Thinking

We optimize for 10 years, not 10 days. We make decisions whose value compounds.

## 10. Pragmatism

We are not dogmatic. When the rules do not fit, we change the rules through process, not by silently violating them.

---

## How Values Are Used

* When in doubt, choose the option that aligns with the values.
* When reviewing a proposal, ask: does this reflect our values?
* When faced with a tradeoff, prefer the option that better preserves a value.
* When values conflict, escalate to a discussion; do not resolve unilaterally.
